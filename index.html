Sculptures
of Scale

The Metropolitan Museum of Art has made nearly 500,000 pieces of art publicly available through its online collection. Anyone with access to a browser can now view the collection’s images virtually. However, when we view these images in a virtual space, we lose the sense of scale we get when we visit the museum in person. This visualization explores a subset of the Met’s online collection in which scale plays a large role in the viewer expereince : sculptures of human subjects.

Get Started

Scultpures of Scale represents a subset of Met’s online collection of sculptures in three categories: large, medium, and small. Click through each category in the Constant Width section to view the sculptures side by side, keeping the width of the images constant. When you’ve selected a sculpture from each category, the Relative Height section below will scale the sculptures, displaying them by their relative heights. Compare the Constant Width and Relative Height sections to explore how scale is represented differently online and in the museum setting.
